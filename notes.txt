Satellite remote sensing frequently relies on infrared (IR) sensors to capture data
 during night time or under adverse weather conditions. However, IR images are inherently 
 monochrome, suffer from low contrast, and lack the rich semantic textures found in visible-spectrum
  (RGB) imagery. This makes it difficult for human analysts and computer vision models to accurately
   identify objects like vehicles, buildings, roads, and vegetation.

This challenge focuses on developing an end-to-end framework that 
simultaneously enhances the structural details of IR satellite images
 and predicts realistic colorization (RGB translation). By transforming 
 low-visibility IR data into high-fidelity, colorized images, this solution
  will drastically improve situational awareness and automated object 
  interpretation.

2. PROBLEM STATEMENT

Satellite remote sensing frequently relies on
 infrared sensors to capture data during nighttime
  or adverse weather conditions. However, infrared images 
  are monochrome, have low contrast, and lack the rich
   textures present in visible RGB images. 
   Because of this, identifying objects such as vehicles,
    buildings, roads, water bodies, and vegetation becomes difficult.

Human analysts and computer vision systems 
often struggle to extract meaningful information from raw infrared images. 
Therefore, there is a need for a system that can enhance infrared imagery 
and convert it into realistic RGB representations while preserving the actual meaning of the scene.

6. PROJECT PIPELINE

Step 1: Data Acquisition

Collect paired infrared and RGB satellite images from Landsat 8/9 datasets.

Step 2: Data Preprocessing

Tasks include:

- Image resizing
- Image normalization
- Noise reduction
- Data preparation

Co-registration:
Infrared and RGB images must be perfectly aligned so that each infrared pixel
 corresponds to the same RGB pixel.

Step 3: Image Enhancement

Goal:
Improve image quality before colorization.

Techniques:

- Sharpening
- Super-resolution

Benefits:

- Clearer edges
- Better texture visibility
- Improved structural details

Step 4: Infrared to RGB Colorization

Input:
Single-channel infrared image

Output:
Colorized RGB image

Possible Models:

- Pix2Pix
- CycleGAN

The model learns mappings such as:

- Water → Blue
- Vegetation → Green
- Urban areas → Natural land colors

Step 5: Semantic Constraint Integration

Purpose:
To ensure meaningful color assignments and prevent incorrect colorization.

Examples:

- Water remains blue.
- Forest regions remain green.
- Roads and buildings retain realistic appearances.

Step 6: Evaluation and Validation

Generated RGB images are compared with actual RGB images to evaluate performance.

7. OBJECTIVES OF THE PROJECT

8. Enhancement via Super-Resolution and Sharpening
   Improve visibility of faint edges and textures in raw infrared imagery.

9. Predict Realistic Colorization
   Convert monochrome infrared images into realistic RGB representations without introducing artifacts.

10. Preserve Semantic Integrity
    Ensure that enhancement and colorization do not alter the true meaning of objects present in the scene.

11. Boost Downstream Tasks
    Improve the accuracy of object detection and image segmentation models.

8. EVALUATION METRICS

9. PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio)

Purpose:
Measures reconstruction quality.

Interpretation:
Higher PSNR values indicate better image quality and closer similarity to the original RGB image.

2. SSIM (Structural Similarity Index)

Purpose:
Measures structural similarity between generated and ground-truth images.

Checks:

- Shapes
- Edges
- Patterns

Interpretation:
Higher SSIM values indicate better preservation of structural information.

3. FID (Fréchet Inception Distance)

Purpose:
Measures realism of generated RGB images.

Interpretation:
Lower FID values indicate more realistic generated images.

4. Inference Time

Purpose:
Measures the time required to process a single satellite image.

Importance:
Ensures scalability for large satellite datasets.

5. Visual Inspection

Human experts evaluate:

- Realism of colors
- Image quality
- Presence of artifacts
- Whether any false or hallucinated objects are generated

---

9. SEMANTIC INTEGRITY

Definition:
Semantic integrity means preserving the actual meaning of objects during enhancement and colorization.

Examples:

- A road should remain a road.
- A river should remain a river.
- A building should remain a building.

Importance:
The model must improve image quality without changing the identity of objects.
